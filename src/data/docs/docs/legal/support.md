## Frequently Asked Questions

### Data & Privacy

**Q: Where is my health data stored?**
Your data is stored encrypted on your device. If sync is enabled, an encrypted blob is sent to our server — we cannot read its contents (zero-knowledge architecture).

**Q: Does FitMe sell my data?**
No. We do not sell, rent, or share your health data with any third party for advertising or commercial purposes.

**Q: How do I delete all my data?**
Go to Settings → Privacy → Delete My Account. All local and synced data is permanently deleted. Synced records are purged from our servers within 30 days.

**Q: How do I export my data?**
Go to Settings → Privacy → Export My Data. Your data is exported as a JSON file you can save or transfer.

**Q: Can I opt out of analytics?**
Yes. Go to Settings → Privacy → Analytics and toggle off. No analytics events will be sent after that point.

---

### Apple HealthKit

**Q: Why does FitMe request HealthKit access?**
FitMe reads workouts, body metrics, HRV, and sleep data from Apple Health to build your daily readiness score. It also writes logged workouts back to Apple Health so your activity stays in sync.

**Q: How do I revoke HealthKit access?**
Open iOS Settings → Health → Data Access & Devices → FitMe, then toggle off any categories you want to revoke.

**Q: Is my HealthKit data shared with anyone?**
No. HealthKit data is used only on-device to compute your readiness score. It is never sent to third parties or used for advertising.

### Account & Sync

**Q: Do I need an account to use FitMe?**
No. The app works fully offline without an account. An account is only required to enable cross-device sync.

**Q: I lost my device — is my data safe?**
Synced data is encrypted with keys derived from your credentials. Without your password, the encrypted blobs on our server cannot be decrypted.

**Q: How do I sign out?**
Go to Settings → Account → Sign Out. Local data remains on the device; synced data stays encrypted on the server until you delete your account.
