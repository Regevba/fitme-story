# Building a Privacy-First Fitness Platform --- Product Requirements

## Context

This is the master product requirements document for FitMe, an iPhone-first fitness app that replaces separate training, nutrition, and recovery apps with a single privacy-first experience. The document covers product strategy, competitive positioning, and a full feature inventory across 11 product areas. It is a living document that has evolved through three major versions as the product shipped.

---

## Part 1: Product Strategy

### Problem Statement

Serious fitness enthusiasts use 3-5 separate apps to track their training, nutrition, recovery, and body composition. This fragmentation creates:

- **Data silos** --- workout data in one app, nutrition in another, sleep in a third. No unified picture of readiness or progress.
- **Decision fatigue** --- users check multiple dashboards to answer a simple question: "What should I do today?"
- **Privacy erosion** --- each app collects health data independently, often uploading raw biometrics to separate cloud services with varying security practices.
- **Inconsistent UX** --- switching between apps with different design languages, interaction patterns, and data entry flows wastes time and breaks flow.

**Who is affected:** Health-conscious individuals who train consistently (3-6 days/week), track macros, and want to understand how their body responds over time. These users are underserved by generic fitness apps that treat training, nutrition, and recovery as unrelated activities.

**Why now:** Apple's ecosystem (HealthKit, Foundation Models, Secure Enclave) enables a privacy-first approach where sensitive health data never leaves the device unencrypted. No competitor combines federated AI intelligence with zero-knowledge encryption in a single native app.

### Vision and Elevator Pitch

**Vision:** FitMe is the iPhone-first fitness command center that unifies training, nutrition, recovery, and body composition into a single privacy-first experience --- powered by federated AI that learns from population patterns while keeping your data encrypted on your device.

**Elevator pitch:** "FitMe replaces your training log, meal tracker, and recovery dashboard with one app that knows what you should do today --- without ever seeing your private health data."

**North star metric:** Weekly Active Users who complete at least one training session AND log at least one meal (cross-feature engagement).

### Target Personas

#### Primary: The Consistent Lifter
- 25-40 years old, trains 4-6 days/week, 1-5 years of structured training experience
- Wants to build muscle, track progressive overload, optimize nutrition for body composition
- Pain points: logging is tedious, can't see how nutrition affects recovery, uses 3+ apps

#### Secondary: The Health-Conscious Professional
- 30-50 years old, trains 3-4 days/week, values efficiency and simplicity
- Wants to maintain fitness, manage weight, improve sleep and recovery
- Pain points: too many screens, too much data entry, wants actionable guidance not raw numbers

#### Tertiary: The Data-Driven Optimizer
- 25-45, deep interest in biomarkers, body composition, and performance trends
- Wants to optimize every variable --- sleep, HRV, nutrition timing, training periodization
- Pain points: existing apps don't cross-reference enough data

### Value Proposition

| FitMe | Competitors |
|-------|-------------|
| One app for training + nutrition + recovery + stats | Separate apps for each |
| Privacy-first: AES-256 encryption, zero-knowledge sync | Cloud storage of raw health data |

**Unique positioning:** "Privacy-first fitness intelligence" --- the only app combining federated cohort AI with zero-knowledge encryption, giving users population-level insights without ever exposing personal health data to any server.

### Go-to-Market Strategy

**Phase 1 --- Beta (TestFlight):** Invite-only beta with 100-500 users. Focus on core training + nutrition loop validation. Collect NPS and feature requests.

**Phase 2 --- App Store Launch:** App Store Optimization, landing page, social proof from beta users, Reddit/fitness community presence.

**Phase 3 --- Growth:** Influencer partnerships, content marketing, referral program, Android launch to expand TAM.

## Part 2: Feature Inventory

FitMe ships 11 product areas. Rather than reproducing every requirement, the table below summarizes what is built, what is planned, and the key differentiator per area.

| Feature Area | Status | Key Capabilities | Differentiator |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Training Tracking** | Shipped | 87 exercises, RPE logging, PR detection, rest timer, progressive overload, cardio with HR zones | Session-by-session comparison with ghost rows |
| **Nutrition Logging** | Shipped | Macro tracking, smart label parsing, meal templates, supplement streaks, dynamic calorie targets | Phase-based adaptive targets tied to body composition goals |
| **Recovery and Biometrics** | Shipped | HealthKit auto-import, manual entry, HRV/RHR/sleep tracking, readiness scoring | Multi-source readiness formula (see [readiness-score-formula.md](readiness-score-formula.md)) |
| **Home / Today Screen** | Shipped | Animated info strip, progress orb, 5-page readiness card, status dots | Single-glance daily command center answering "What should I do today?" |
| **Stats / Progress Hub** | Shipped | 18 metrics, 5 time periods, multi-chart types, data source attribution | Coverage summary with source badges (Watch, Scale, Manual, HealthKit) |
| **Authentication** | Shipped | Apple Sign In, passkeys/WebAuthn, email/password, biometric lock | Post-login biometric lock with Secure Enclave key protection |
| **Settings** | Shipped | 5 category groups, unit system, appearance, sync status, customizable thresholds | Readiness threshold and Zone 2 HR range configuration |
| **Data and Sync** | Shipped | AES-256-GCM encryption, CloudKit + Supabase sync, zero-knowledge architecture | Servers store only `.ftenc` ciphertext --- never see raw health data |
| **AI / Cohort Intelligence** | Shipped | 3-tier pipeline, 4 recommendation segments, privacy-preserving bands, k-anonymity | Federated AI with k=50 minimum cohort size (see [ai-engine.md](ai-engine.md)) |
| **Design System** | Shipped | 90+ semantic tokens, 13 reusable components, token pipeline with CI gate | Three-tier architecture: primitives, semantic tokens, component consumption |
| **Onboarding** | Shipped | Welcome, goal setup, profile capture, consent framing, HealthKit permission, skip option | Permission requests with context, not blind system prompts |

## Key Takeaways

1. **Privacy as product strategy, not compliance checkbox.** Zero-knowledge encryption and federated AI are architectural decisions that shape every feature, not bolted-on privacy policies.
2. **Data-driven kill criteria.** Every feature defines not just success metrics but explicit conditions for abandonment. This prevents feature bloat and ensures the team kills what isn't working.
3. **Cross-feature engagement as the north star.** Rather than optimizing a single feature (workouts logged, meals tracked), the north star requires users to engage across domains --- training AND nutrition --- which validates the unification thesis.
4. **Progressive platform expansion.** iPhone-first with a clear priority stack (Android, Watch, iPad, Web) rather than trying to ship everywhere simultaneously.
